About The Disease

Knee Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease caused by the gradual breakdown of cartilage, leading to pain, stiffness, swelling, and reduced mobility. It is one of the most common musculoskeletal disorders, particularly in adults, and often worsens over time if not properly managed.

Globally, knee OA affects more than 500 million people, representing about 7% of the population. Women are disproportionately impacted, and between 1990 and 2019, cases rose by 48%. By 2019, knee OA accounted for 2% of all years lived with disability (YLDs), ranking it among the top 15 global causes of disability.

Our Mission

Our mission is to leverage Artificial Intelligence to provide accurate and timely detection of Knee Osteoarthritis. Early diagnosis empowers individuals and healthcare professionals to take preventive and corrective measures before the disease severely impacts quality of life.

By combining medical imaging with AI, we aim to reduce diagnostic delays, support clinicians, and make advanced healthcare solutions accessible to a larger population.

Why We Chose This

Knee Osteoarthritis is not only a medical challenge but also a global public health concern. With rising cases due to aging populations, sedentary lifestyles, and obesity, there is a growing need for early detection tools.

We chose this project because it lies at the intersection of healthcare and technology, providing an opportunity to make a meaningful difference. By developing an AI-based system, we hope to contribute towards reducing disability, improving patient outcomes, and supporting medical professionals with faster, data-driven decisions.
